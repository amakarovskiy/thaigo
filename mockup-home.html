Questions people ask

We understand it can be
hard to ask

Will my privacy be protected? +
Completely. We do not share any information about residents with anyone outside our team without explicit consent. Many of our residents value this above everything else.
How long does treatment take? +
Programmes typically run from 4 weeks to 6 months, depending on the person and the nature of the dependency. We'll recommend what we genuinely believe is right for the individual — never what's most convenient for us.
What if my loved one refuses to go? +
This is very common. Call us anyway — we can guide you through how to have the conversation, what to do and what not to do, and what options exist when someone is not yet ready to seek help.
Can family visit during treatment? +
Yes — family involvement is a core part of our programme. We structure visits carefully so they support recovery rather than disrupting it. Family therapy sessions are also available.
What happens after leaving the centre? +
We don't stop at discharge. Every resident leaves with an aftercare plan — online therapy, support groups, follow-up sessions with their therapist. Recovery is a long road; we stay on it with you.
Do you treat addiction and mental health together? +
Yes. Dual diagnosis — addiction alongside depression, anxiety, trauma, or other conditions — is something we specialise in. Treating only one part of the picture rarely leads to lasting recovery.
